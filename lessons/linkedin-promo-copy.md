## Post Option 1 (Primary)

I wrote the guide I wish existed when I started integrating AI into real work.

Not the "10x your productivity" version. The one where you've shipped something AI wrote and realized three days later it was wrong. Or where you spent more time editing AI output than it would've taken to write it yourself. Or where you quietly noticed you're getting worse at the thing you used to be great at.

I put together 8 workflows I actually use — the patterns that hold up when the stakes are real, the deadline is tomorrow, and "good enough" isn't.

The AI Playbook covers:

→ The Vetting Loop — catching plausible-but-wrong output before it ships
→ Constraint-First Prompting — why telling AI what NOT to do matters more
→ Research + Synthesis — using AI to organize what you've learned, not learn for you
→ Skill Preservation — automating the toil without eroding what makes you valuable
→ The Quality Check — why review is real work, not a formality
→ Iteration Depth vs. Speed — 3 versions of excellent beats 15 versions of mediocre
→ Knowledge Hygiene — managing uncertainty honestly
→ Augmentation, Not Automation — building workflows that survive the edge cases

Free. No email gate. No signup.

30 years of strategy. The last few years of figuring out where AI actually helps — and where it quietly makes you worse at your job.
