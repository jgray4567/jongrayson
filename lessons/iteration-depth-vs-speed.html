The AI Playbook · Lesson 6 of 8

Iteration Depth vs. Speed

15 variations of mediocre isn't progress

The Problem

AI makes iteration fast, so you iterate many times. But speed doesn't equal depth. You end up with 15 variations of mediocre instead of 3 versions of excellent.

The Workflow
Step 1

Define the Test

What are you actually testing? Voice? Structure? Approach?

Step 2

2–3 Variations

Create variations with different strategic assumptions.

Step 3

Evaluate vs. Goal

Judge against the goal, not 'which do I like more.'

Step 4

Pick One

Commit to a direction. Stop searching.

Step 5

Iterate Depth

Now refine, detail, and strengthen the chosen direction.

Why This Works

You're iterating toward something, not iterating to avoid deciding. AI's speed becomes a feature only when you're disciplined about why you're iterating.

Real-World Scenario

Your team is choosing between centralized, federated, or hybrid approaches. AI can generate 20 variations of each — that's noise. Instead, generate one serious version that answers the strategic question. Evaluate against outcomes. Pick one. Then deepen with AI's help.

Watch For
Using iteration speed to avoid upfront thinking
Treating 'more options' as 'better options'
Optimizing for quantity of variations instead of depth
